Where Bonusly is actually better

The parts we're not going to pretend about.

We built Bryte Rewards, so of course we think we're a better fit for most people. But Bonusly has been doing this since 2012, and in these specific places, they're ahead of us.

B

They have way more customers

4,000+ vs our 340+. If "which tool does everyone use?" is your primary decision criterion, the answer is them. (We'd argue it shouldn't be — but we also get it.)

B

Deeper analytics & reporting

Their admin dashboards are more mature. Ten years of product surface will do that. We ship fast, but they have cohort analysis and segment drill-downs we haven't built yet.

B

Gamification depth

If your culture thrives on points races, streak leaderboards, and public scoring — they've built that in a way we deliberately haven't. Different philosophy, theirs is the one you want.

B

Bigger global rewards catalogue

Because of scale, they can source from more U.S./international aggregators. If you have a team across 40 countries, their catalogue is broader. (Though for Canadian teams specifically, ours is richer.)

B

A more battle-tested product

They've been in production since 2012. We've been live since 2024. Some of our edges are shinier; some of theirs have had ten years of edge-case polish.

The honest recommendation

Here's who should pick which tool.

Choose Bryte Rewards if…

  • You're a Canadian team, or a U.S. team with a significant Canadian office.
  • Your team is 50–2,000 people. We're laser-focused on this band.
  • You care more about recognition quality than recognition volume.
  • You want data residency in Canada by default — not a $2k/mo add-on.
  • You've tried gamified points systems and found they flatten the culture rather than build it.
  • You want bilingual EN/FR that actually works — not a half-done translation.
  • You want honest, flat pricing with nothing locked behind "Enterprise Plus."
  • You prefer a small, design-forward team over a mature marketplace leader.

Choose Bonusly if…

  • You're a U.S. team and data residency is not a factor.
  • You're 2,000+ employees and want a product with a decade of enterprise polish.
  • Your culture thrives on gamification — streaks, leaderboards, points races.
  • You need extremely deep analytics / slicing across huge datasets.
  • Your team is genuinely global (20+ countries) and catalogue breadth matters more than Canadian depth.
  • You're replacing an existing Bonusly deployment and the switching cost outweighs the gains.
  • You prefer a bigger vendor with a marketplace footprint.
