01
Data protection

Encrypted everywhere. Always.

Every byte, at rest and in transit. No caveats, no "except for this service", no legacy endpoints hiding in staging.

AES-256 encryption at rest
All customer data — recognitions, comments, uploaded avatars — encrypted with AWS KMS-managed keys. Keys rotate every 90 days.
TLS 1.3 in transit
Perfect forward secrecy. HSTS preloaded. No TLS 1.0/1.1 accepted anywhere in our stack.
Tenant isolation with row-level security
Every query is scoped to your organisation at the database level — not just the application layer. Misconfiguration can't leak data between customers.
Daily encrypted backups, 30-day retention
Point-in-time recovery to any second in the last 7 days. Quarterly restore drills — we've actually tested this.
02
Access & identity

Your IT team's shortest vendor review.

SSO, SCIM, audit logs, and a permissions model your security reviewer will actually like.

SAML & OIDC single sign-on
Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, JumpCloud, OneLogin. Enforce MFA at the IdP and we'll honour it.
SCIM provisioning
Automatic user lifecycle — add, move, deactivate. When someone leaves, they're out of Bryte Rewards in under 60 seconds.
Granular role-based permissions
Six built-in roles — Employee, Manager, Team Admin, HR Admin, Billing, Super Admin — plus custom scopes. The rewards budget is separate from user data is separate from audit access.
Immutable audit logs, 7 years
Every admin action, every permission change, every export — logged with actor, timestamp, IP, and before/after state. Exportable to SIEM via webhook.

Operational security

The humans behind the product.

The best crypto in the world doesn't help if someone's laptop gets stolen at a Tim's. Here's how we handle the boring, essential part.

Background checks, every hire
Criminal record check and reference verification before an offer is extended. Repeated every 3 years for anyone with production access.
Device management via Kandji / Intune
FileVault / BitLocker enforced. Auto-lock at 5 minutes. Remote wipe on loss. No local customer data stored on laptops — ever.
Least-privilege production access
Engineering access to production is break-glass only — requires a ticket, a second approver, and is time-boxed to 4 hours. All commands logged.

Questions your InfoSec team will ask

Answered, up front.

Can we get a copy of your SOC 2 report? +
Yes. Send your NDA — or use ours — to security@brytecanada.ca and you'll have the full Type II report within one business day. Our auditor is Schellman and our current report covers the trust service criteria for Security, Availability, and Confidentiality.
What happens to our data if we churn? +
You own your data. On termination we provide a full export (JSON + CSV) within 7 days, and hard-delete all production + backup copies within 30 days. A deletion certificate is issued on request. No "soft-delete for analytics" — it's gone.
Do you train AI models on our data? +
No. Your recognition content is never used to train any model — ours, OpenAI's, Anthropic's, anyone's. When we use LLMs for features like the writing assistant, calls are scoped per-request with zero retention contracts. You can disable AI features org-wide with one toggle.
Can you sign our standard DPA? +
Yes. We'll sign yours, or you can use ours — available at brytecanada.ca/legal/dpa. Standard contractual clauses included for any cross-border processing (which we don't do by default, but the clauses are there for completeness).
How do you handle requests from law enforcement? +
We require a Canadian court order or equivalent legal instrument. We notify the affected customer before disclosure unless explicitly prohibited by law, and we publish an annual transparency report. To date: zero requests complied with.
Do you have cyber insurance? +
Yes — $10M CAD in cyber liability coverage through Sovereign Insurance. Certificate available on request.
Can we self-host Bryte Rewards? +
Not today, and honestly — we don't plan to. We've been able to hit the security bars of Crown corporations, major credit unions, and provincial health authorities on our hosted multi-tenant product. If you have a hard self-host requirement, let's talk — we've partnered with on-prem-first customers before on dedicated-tenant deployments.
Who actually runs security at Bryte Rewards? +
Our VP Engineering doubles as our CISO. We have a 3-person security & platform team, supplemented by an always-on engagement with Bishop Fox for pen testing (annually) and a continuous bug-bounty program via HackerOne. A real human will be on your vendor review call.
